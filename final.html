Way back Wednesday

Game 6 of the 1998 NBA Finals

Chicago Bulls Vs. Utah Jazz at the Delta Center in Salt Lake City, Utah.
Game 6 of the 1998 NBA Finals was a professional basketball game that took place on June 14, 1998 between the visiting Chicago Bulls and Utah Jazz. The Bulls won by one point, 87–86!This win was their sixth NBA championship in eight years. Game 6 was Michael Jordan and coach Phil Jackson's last NBA game, they were to both retire, but then eventually come back. This game earned the highest TV ratings of an NBA game of all time. With 5.2 seconds left, Michael Jordan hit a jump shot to give the Bulls an 87–86 lead. Chicago then held onto the win after John Stockton missed a 3-point field goal with barely a second left in the game.

Saturday December, 3rd

Boston Celtics Vs. Philadelphia 76ers

Boston Celtics Vs. Philadelphia 76ers at the Wells Fargo Center in Philadelphia.
A professional basketball game that took place on Saturday December, 3rd. Isaiah Thomas scored 12 straight Celtics points late in the fourth quarter and a season-high 37 in all as Boston beat the short-handed Philadelphia 76ers 107-106 on Saturday night. The 76ers led by as many as 11 points in the opening half before taking a 53-45 lead into the break. The key was 3-point shooting. Philadelphia was 9 for 18 from the arc in the opening 24 minutes while the Celtics were 3 for 11.

Wednesday December, 7th

Los Angeles lakers Vs. Houston Rockets

Lakers Vs. Rockets at the Toyota Center.
A professional basketball game that took place on Wednesday December, 7rd. Eric Gordon made a career-high eight 3-points and scored 26 points to help the Houston Rockets cruise to a 134-95 victory over the Los Angeles Lakers on Wednesday night. The win was a complete shock to everyone watching; over a 30 point lead. The Rockets were up by 12 in the third quarter, then scored 22 points versus the 6 that the Lakers made to make it 96-68 and put the game out of reach with only 3 minutes left in the quarter. Houston made four 3-pointers and got a nifty one-handed dunk from Clint Capela in that run to pad the lead.
